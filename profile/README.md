# Market Systems Lab

Hi! We are Market Systems Lab team

This GitHub profile contains tools, bots, research scripts, analytics systems and infrastructure developed across several years of work with DeFi protocols, on-chain data, market monitoring, automation workflows, strategy research and prediction market operations.

---

## Areas of Work

### Web3 & DeFi Research

Research of DeFi protocols, yield strategies, ve-models, staking mechanics, liquidity incentives and protocol-specific risk factors.

### Prediction Markets & Market Making

Active work with prediction markets such as Polymarket, Predict.fun and other event-based trading venues.

This includes market making, liquidity provision, order book monitoring, pricing analysis, event-driven research, market resolution analysis, latency-sensitive reactions and risk management across binary and multi-outcome markets.

### On-chain Analytics

Tools and systems for extracting, parsing and aggregating blockchain data, wallet activity, transaction histories, protocol interactions, balances and eligibility conditions.

### Trading & Market Infrastructure

Tools for market monitoring, exchange integrations, price/funding data collection, portfolio state tracking, execution support and latency-sensitive analytics.

### Bot Infrastructure

Telegram/Discord bots, monitoring agents, task runners, wallet/account checkers, data exporters and workflow automation tools.

### Quantitative Research

Scripts and systems for strategy backtesting, event-based analysis, market reaction studies, yield comparison, risk/reward estimation and historical behavior analysis.

### Risk Management

Tools for tracking positions, exposures, protocol risks, liquidation conditions, changing market parameters, market-making inventory and operational failure states.

## Repository Status

Most repositories are published as archived portfolio code.

Some projects may contain outdated dependencies, deprecated APIs or assumptions that were valid only during a specific market period.

Unless explicitly stated otherwise, repositories should be treated as examples of previous applied work rather than production-ready software.
